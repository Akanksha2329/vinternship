When I began the Pinternship with the VLED Lab, I did not start with excitement alone. I started with overwhelm.

I am currently in my final year, and at the same time this internship began, I was already handling mid semester exams, upcoming end semester exams, placement activities in college, academic projects, and many other responsibilities. Adding an internship on top of everything felt heavy. There were moments when I genuinely questioned whether I had taken on more than I could handle.

In the initial days, everything felt intense. New platforms, new expectations, and a pace that demanded consistency. It was not that the work was beyond my ability, but it required presence and commitment at a time when my mind was already scattered. There were days when I doubted myself and wondered if I was even capable enough to continue.

At times, it honestly felt like I might not be able to keep going.

What helped me push through was not a sudden confidence boost, but reassurance that came in small yet meaningful ways. Listening to Sudarshan sir during the sessions gave me a different perspective. His words and motivation reminded me that learning is not about perfection or speed, but about staying sincere and showing up regularly. That thought slowly reduced the pressure I had placed on myself.

Equally important was the spirit of my peers. Watching others navigate their own doubts, ask questions without hesitation, and continue despite confusion made me realize that struggling is a shared experience. It made me reflect on how often we assume others are doing fine while questioning our own abilities.

This internship is still ongoing, and I am still learning and figuring things out. There are moments of confusion even now, but I am gradually learning to trust the process and my ability to adapt. I am understanding that progress does not have to be perfect to be meaningful.

The Pinternship experience so far has been more than just learning new skills. It is teaching me resilience, patience, and how to keep going even when things feel overwhelming. I am continuing this journey with a more grounded mindset and an openness to learn, grow, and improve with each step.
